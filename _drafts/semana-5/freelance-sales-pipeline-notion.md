Think about the last three potential clients you spoke with who didn't end up hiring you. Did you follow up after sending the proposal? Did you write to them a week later to see if they had questions?

If the answer is "not always" or "I forgot," that's not a motivation problem. It's a system problem.

## Why freelancers lose leads

When you're in the middle of several active projects, managing potential clients takes a back seat. You send the proposal and wait. If they don't respond in a few days, the lead disappears from your radar.

But sales statistics are clear: most proposals don't close on first contact. The difference between a freelancer who closes well and one who doesn't is often in the follow-up.

## What a pipeline is and why you need one

A sales pipeline is simply a visualization of where each potential client stands in your commercial process. From first contact to hiring.

Typical stages for a freelancer:

1. **Lead** — someone has shown interest
2. **Initial contact** — you've had a first conversation
3. **Proposal sent** — you've sent a quote or proposal
4. **Negotiation** — they're evaluating, there are questions or price negotiation
5. **Closed won** — hired
6. **Closed lost** — didn't work out (and why)

With this in Kanban format in Notion, you see at a glance how many leads you have in each phase and which ones have been stuck without movement the longest.

## The automatic follow-up tracking that changes things

Add a "Last contact" field to your prospects database. Every time you interact with a lead, update that date.

Then create a formula "Days without contact" = today - last contact.

Sort your view by that column. The leads that have gone longest without contact rise to the top. That becomes your follow-up list for the day.

You don't need to remember who you need to write to. Notion tells you.

## What to note when you lose a client

The "Closed lost" column is one of the most valuable if you use it well. Add a note about why it didn't close: price too high, bad timing, they chose someone else, the project was cancelled.

Over time, that data tells you useful things. If 80% of your lost proposals are about price, you may need to adjust how you present your value. If it's about timing, you may need to generate leads further in advance.
