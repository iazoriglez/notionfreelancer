The Notion template marketplace has thousands of options. And most share the same problem: they're designed to look good in a screenshot, not to solve a freelancer's real problems.

After testing dozens of them, here's my honest analysis.

## What makes a Notion template genuinely useful

A useful template solves a specific problem, is easy to adapt to your way of working, and doesn't require an hour of setup before you can use it.

Templates that don't work are the ones with 20 interconnected databases for scenarios nobody will ever use, or the ones so generic they don't fit any real workflow.

## The categories that deliver most value to freelancers

**CRM and client management** — This is the highest-impact category for a freelancer. Having clients, projects, and invoices related to each other completely changes how you run your business. Look for templates with related databases, not flat lists.

**Project tracking** — A Kanban view of projects with custom statuses (not just "to do / doing / done") and fields for delivery dates and assigned client. Simple but powerful.

**Time tracking** — If you charge by the hour, you need a time log integrated with your projects. Templates that do this well save a lot of headaches at billing time.

**Weekly dashboard** — A home page that shows you the most important things for the week: tasks, upcoming deliveries, outstanding invoices. If you have to search for that information, the template isn't well designed.

## What to avoid

Templates with many nested pages and subpages that become a maze. Ones with text fields where there should be databases. Ones that don't allow linking information between sections.

And above all: templates that are pretty but empty. A project management template without calculated fields is just a table you could build in Excel in five minutes.

## The template I most recommend to start with

If you can only choose one, choose a good CRM template. It has the most day-to-day impact for a freelancer because it centralizes the information you constantly use: who your clients are, where your projects stand, what invoices you have pending.
